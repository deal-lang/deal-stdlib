The one change: acre

The file originally carried 4046.86, which rounds the international acre. The US survey acre is 4046.873 m² — a ~4 ppm difference. Updated to the exact international value 4046.8564224 with a doc comment recording the distinction (international has been the US standard since the 2023 survey-foot deprecation). All other factors were already correct to the precision written; no other edits.

§5 Observations (no action required)

  • Pre-existing Phase-4 imperial precision. lb = 0.453592 and oz = 0.028350 (authored in Phase 4) round the exact avoirdupois pound 0.45359237 kg at 6 s.f. Correct as written; could be tightened to full precision in a future pass, but these are baseline values outside Lane B's scope.
  • BTU flavor. BTU = 1055.06 (Phase 4) matches the IT British thermal unit (1055.056 J). The thermochemical BTU (1054.35 J) differs — the IT value is the conventional engineering choice and is correctly used.
  • Constant call-form. Encoding constants as c(1) unit-style defs is a representation choice (parse-safety), not a numeric concern — values are exact regardless of surface form.
